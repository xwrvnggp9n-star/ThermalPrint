ThermalPrint

Print photos on an MXW01 Bluetooth thermal printer, right from your Mac.

Download for macOS Free · Apple silicon · macOS 12 or later · all releases

Features

True print preview

The preview is the exact 384-pixel, 1-bit bitmap the printer receives — what you see is what prints.

Dialed-in dithering

Floyd–Steinberg dithering with darkness, brightness, and contrast controls for readable photos on thermal paper.

Rotate & mirror

Turn the image in 90° steps or flip it left-to-right with one click, right on the preview.

In the Share menu

Send a picture straight from Photos, Finder, or Safari — ThermalPrint opens with it loaded and ready.

Drag, drop, print

Drop a photo on the window or the Dock icon. The printer is found automatically and remembered.

Signed & self-updating

Developer ID signed and notarized by Apple; the app checks for updates and installs them in place.

iPhone & iPad

ThermalPrint goes mobile

The same true-to-paper preview and dialed-in dithering, now in your pocket. Choose a photo, see the exact 1-bit bitmap the printer will produce, and print over Bluetooth — no account, no sign-in, no data collection.

Coming soon to the App Store

Compatible printers

ThermalPrint works with the inexpensive 384-pixel Bluetooth “cat printer” thermal printers built on the MXW01 board — the ones that show up over Bluetooth with a name beginning MXW01- (e.g. MXW01-1A2B). These are sold under many generic brand names online, typically for around $20–30, and use 57 mm thermal paper.

Command-line tools (mxprint) are included in the source repository.
